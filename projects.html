Dynamic Wealth:

Working at the Aresty Research Center alongside Dr. Sopher, we were concerned with developing a way of determining optimal portfolio ceilings in order to maximize wealth generation in a dynamic process. Utilizing Monte Carlo simulations, we were able to quickly run thousands of runs on several different parameterizations. I then used what we learned to create a survey to test people's ability to accurately place these ceilings themselves and compare them to the optimized values we arrived at through our Monte Carlo simulations.

AudioMac:

Trading interviews are HARD. On top of all the advanced mathematics there is to nail, there is often a mental math portion that forces candidates to think quickly and accuratey, simulating life on the trading floor! AudioMac is derived from the drilling tool Zetamac, but with a fun twist-questions are asked verbally via a device's speakers and the user is able to respond back verbally, better simulating an interview experience! Free to use, and customizable, AudioMac is a perfect drilling tool for anyone preparing for interviews or wanting to keep a sharp mind!

The Prosperity Index:

I was lucky enough to be invited to J.P. Morgan Chase's 2023 Data for Good event! It was a fantastic experience leveraging my technical skills for the common good. My team and I were tasked with ingesting over 240,000 individual pieces of health, education, crime and economic data for states across the U.S. to identify 3 target cities that the non profit organization we were partnered with could expand to. We utilized a k means clustering algorithm and were able to determine 3 perfect locations! The judges were impressed with our work and we were able to take home 3rd place!
